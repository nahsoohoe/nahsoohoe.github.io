Cameron Seniors' Website  ( www.cameron55plus.org )

S.H. Nah, June 10, 2009

As more and more seniors are becoming computer and Internet literate, a website for their society will be useful for them.  The website can provide, at the very least, the following benefits:
  • timely information about activities and events
  • latest news on special events and notices
  • a convenient means to provide feedback
  • a means of inculcating and/or encouraging an online culture as the website can serve as an online entity that they can readily identify and interact with
  • a forum to discuss items and topics of mutual interest
The website is not intended to replace the current printed newsletter, rather it complements it.

Design Criteria
Since many seniors may not be very familiar with accessing websites and using web services, in the design of the website, the following principles are followed:
  • the design should be simple and "to the point"
  • the information should not be provided in a complex manner
  • complex navigation and excessive multimedia/graphics are discouraged
In order that at a later stage volunteers can be engaged to input content and possibly manage the website, it should be created using tools and/or software that can facilitate this.

Phases of the Website
It is intended that the website starts off as a simple one serving mainly to disseminate information and as time goes on, if it proves to be popular, it can be enhanced to provide more functionalities and interactivity.

Phase 1
  • Main function is to disseminate information about the society
  • Content is mainly static
  • All content accessible to the public
  • Interactivity limited to email feedback forms
  • Content is created and maintained mainly by the webmaster
Phase 2
  • Creation of registered members' section and content.  These may include:
    • dissemination of members-only information
    • forums and discussion groups
    • members mailing list
    • provision of email addresses to members if they need it
    • posting and updating of content by volunteers
    • members blog section
  • Sections of the website provided for specific activities
